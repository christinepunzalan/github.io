GENEVA

Geneva is the second-largest city in Switzerland. The French-speaking city is famous as “World’s Smallest Metropolis” and situated on the banks of Lake Geneva, right at the foot of the Swiss Alps.

ZURICH

Zurich, being located in the heart of Switzerland and in the center of Europe, is one of the most important financial centers in the world. Zurich is considered as the highest standard of living in the world.

Interlaken is a traditional resort town in the mountainous Bernese Oberland region of central Switzerland. This place lies between Thun and Brienz lakes, hence the name of the city originates.

Grossmünster

This is a prominent Protestant church in Zurich, notable for its twin towers and significant role in the Swiss Reformation led by Huldrych Zwingli in the early 16th century. The Romanesque-style building dates back to around the 12th century and is a key symbol of Zurich.

Palace of Nations

Located in Geneva, this building serves as the European headquarters of the United Nations. Built in the 1930s, it was originally home to the League of Nations. The palace is known for hosting numerous diplomatic meetings and is surrounded by parkland with views of Lake Geneva.
